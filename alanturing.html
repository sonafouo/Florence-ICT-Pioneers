The Enigma Codebreaker

Biography

Alan Turing made history for his groundbreaking contributions to computer science, artificial intelligence, cryptography, and mathematics. He was a national hero, who used his skills in World War II to crack the Enigma code.

Alan Turing is known as the father of computer science. Turing's work laid the foundation for modern computing and artificial intelligence.

Alan Turing is known as the father of computer science.

Key Contributions

  • Developed the concept of the Turing machine, a model of a general-purpose computer
  • Played a crucial role in breaking the Nazi Enigma code during World War II
  • Pioneered the field of artificial intelligence with the Turing Test

Timeline

Year Event
1912 Born in London, England
1936 Published "On Computable Numbers" introducing the Turing machine
1939-1945 Worked at Bletchley Park, breaking the Enigma code
1950 Introduced the Turing Test for artificial intelligence
1954 Passed away at the age of 41
